Vortex Offsite Agenda

Day 1: Streamlining Onboarding/Account Management, Product Focus & Packages, Personality Insights, Feedback & Massage Surprise

Morning

  • Welcome & Icebreaker: "Two Truths and a Lie - Google Cloud Edition"
    • Objective: Fun, gets people talking, and starts the day on a positive, relevant note.
  • Customer Onboarding & Account Management: Problem Deep Dive
    • Focus: A focused session to identify pain points in the current onboarding and account management process.
    • Process:
      1. Individual Reflection: Each person silently lists their top 3-5 challenges in each area (onboarding and account management).
      2. Round Robin Sharing: Each person shares their challenges, one at a time, while someone captures them on the whiteboard, grouping similar issues.
      3. Prioritization: The team uses dot voting (each person gets 3 votes) to identify the top 3-5 most critical challenges to address.
      4. Root Cause Analysis: For each prioritized challenge, brainstorm potential root causes using a "5 Whys" approach.
  • Solution Design: Onboarding & Account Management
    • Activity: Collaborative session to design solutions to the prioritized challenges.
    • Process:
      1. Brainstorming Solutions: For each prioritized challenge, brainstorm potential solutions. Encourage "out-of-the-box" thinking.
      2. Solution Selection: As a group, select the most promising solution(s) for each challenge.
      3. Process Mapping: Develop a detailed process flow for the improved onboarding and account management processes, including specific steps, responsible parties, and timelines.
      4. Checklist/Template Creation: Based on the process flow, create checklists or templates to standardize the new processes.

Afternoon

  • Team Lunch at Monínec Restaurant
  • Product Focus & Unique Vortex Packages
    • Activity: Deep dive into Google Cloud products and how to package them for better sales, focusing on Vortex's strengths.
    • Focus Areas:
      1. Description of Focus Areas:
        • Identify and discuss the areas where Vortex has the strongest expertise and experience with Google Cloud products.
        • Analyze the specific Google Cloud products where Vortex excels (e.g., specific areas of compute, storage, databases, AI/ML, etc.).
        • Discuss the industries or types of clients where Vortex has been most successful.
        • Define what makes Vortex's knowledge and experience unique in these areas.
        • Set goals - where do you want to improve and grow
      2. Unique Vortex Package Creation:
        • Brainstorm innovative ways to bundle Google Cloud products and Vortex's services into unique and attractive packages.
        • Create packages based on the identified focus areas where Vortex has a competitive advantage.
        • Consider creating tiered packages (e.g., Essential, Advanced, Enterprise) to address various client needs and budgets.
        • Define clear and compelling value propositions for each package, emphasizing Vortex's unique expertise and how it benefits the client.
      3. Sales Strategies:
        • Discuss how to effectively position and sell these unique packages to clients.
        • Identify potential target audiences for each package (consider industry, company size, specific needs).
        • Develop sales enablement materials (e.g., one-pagers, presentations, case studies) that highlight the value of Vortex's expertise and the unique packages.
  • Personalized Feedback & Personality Insights
    • Activity: Taking a personality test, sharing feedback, and discussing results.
    • Part 1: Personality Insights (15 minutes)
      • Activity:
        1. Each team member takes the 16Personalities test.
        2. Allow for a few minutes of silent reflection.
    • Part 2: Feedback Sharing (75 minutes)
      • Process:
        1. Each team member prepares written feedback for their colleagues beforehand, focusing on:
          • Strengths: What does this person do exceptionally well? What are their key contributions to the team?
          • Areas for Growth: What are 1-2 specific things this person could do differently to be even more effective? Provide constructive examples.
        2. Feedback is shared in a round-robin format. One person receives feedback at a time.
        3. The person receiving feedback listens actively, asks clarifying questions, and thanks their colleagues for their input.
    • Part 3: Discussion (15 minutes)
      • Each person shares their personality type (if comfortable) and insights gained.
      • Discuss how different personality types might interact within the team and how understanding these differences can improve communication and collaboration.
  • 5:00 PM: Surprise Gift: Massage
    • Each team member receives a 40-minute massage, scheduled individually throughout the evening.
  • Day 1 Wrap-Up & Action Item Review

Evening

  • Team Dinner & Small Party at Monínec

Day 2: Sales Pitch Mastery & Efficient Meetings Design, implementation of Day1 ideas

Morning

  • Team Breakfast
  • Sales Pitch Challenge: "Selling Vortex's Value"
    • Each team member delivers a 5-minute sales pitch highlighting Vortex's services.
    • Preparation: Prior to the offsite, share the judging criteria.
    • Sales Pitch Criteria (Vortex Services):
      • Clear Value Proposition
      • Compelling Storytelling
      • Product/Service Knowledge
      • Delivery & Confidence
    • Judging: There is no judgement! Or is it?
    • Feedback: After each pitch, provide 2-3 minutes of constructive feedback.

Afternoon

  • Team Lunch
  • Efficient Meetings Design: Sales & Engineering Syncs
    • Creating ideal meeting structures for new customer acquisition and account management.
    • Part 1: New Customer Acquisition Meetings
    • Part 2: Account Management Meetings
    • Key Principles for Both Meeting Types: Mandatory Agenda, Timeboxing, Action-Oriented, Rotating Roles.
  • Offsite Wrap-Up, Action Planning, & Feedback
    • Review action items, assign owners and deadlines, and gather feedback on the offsite.
